Projects

Practical, community-led work that reduces illness, grows local capacity, and improves access to care.

Community education Clean water Medical jornadas Midwife training

Community health education

Six-month courses led by local educators and visiting health professionals. Topics include hygiene and sanitation, nutrition and cooking, wound care, first aid, and women’s health.

Clean water access

In partnership with Ecofiltros, we provide affordable ceramic water filters and hands-on training in setup, maintenance, and safe storage so families can drink safely every day.

Medical jornadas

Mobile clinics bring primary care to several communities in the Chicacao region, staffed by Guatemalan providers with support from clinicians in the United States. We focus on patient education, prevention, and rapid connection to local services.

Midwife training

With local ministries of health we strengthen midwifery skills using Laerdal’s Helping Babies Survive curriculum to improve outcomes for mothers and newborns.
